Understanding Stimulants

üß† How They Work

Most stimulants increase dopamine and norepinephrine levels in the brain by blocking their reuptake or promoting their release. This leads to increased alertness, energy, and often euphoria.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Risks

  • Cardiovascular strain: Increased heart rate and blood pressure
  • Sleep disruption: Insomnia and sleep debt
  • Tolerance: Rapid development requiring higher doses
  • Dependency: Physical and psychological addiction potential
  • Hyperthermia: Dangerous overheating, especially with dancing/exercise

üõ°Ô∏è Harm Reduction

  • Start low: Begin with minimal doses to assess sensitivity
  • Stay hydrated: But don't over-hydrate - electrolyte balance matters
  • Eat regularly: Stimulants suppress appetite but nutrition is crucial
  • Sleep scheduling: Plan around the duration to avoid sleep debt
  • Temperature monitoring: Be aware of overheating, especially in hot environments
